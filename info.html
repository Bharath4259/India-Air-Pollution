Terminology

SO2

Sulfur dioxide (SO2), a colorless, bad-smelling, toxic gas, is part of a larger group of chemicals referred to as sulfur oxides (SOx). The main source of sulfur dioxide in the air is industrial activity or by burning coal, oil or deisel or other materials that contain sulfur.

NO2

Nitrogen dioxide NO2, is a gaseous air pollutant composed of nitrogen and oxygen. NO2 primarily gets in the air from the burning of fuel. NO2 forms from emissions from vehicles, power plants, and off-road equipment. Its presence in air contributes to the formation and modification of other air pollutants, such as ozone and particulate matter, and to acid rain.

RSPM

Respirable Suspended Particulate Matter (RSPM) is considered as particulate matter with their diameter (aerodynamic) less than 2.5 micrometers. This is a causative agent of mortality and morbidity. These Small particles aggravate respiratory and cardiac symptoms in the short term and trigger lung cancer in the long term.

SPM

Suspended Particulate Matter (SPM) in air is considered a criteria to indicate air quality. Various allergens, fibrous materials, heavy metals and even many organic carcinogens are present in air in the form of suspended particulates.
